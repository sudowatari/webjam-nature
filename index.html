Poppy

The poppy flower is the symbol of relaxation and recovery. It is believed that this flower can help you in treating many health problems and it has a calming effect of nerves. The symbolism of the poppy flower as a calming flower comes from ancient times. In many cultures all over the world this flower is perceived as a symbol of remembrance, as well as a symbol of consolation and resurrection. In some parts of the world the poppy flower is related to beauty and luxury. In the past this flower was used only by rich people and it was a part of their luxurious lifestyle.

Chamomile

The humble Chamomile does more than just make a good cup of tea. It’s also full of symbolism that has been used by artists throughout the centuries. Chamomile flowers carry the symbolic meaning of rest, peace, poise, calmness. The flower is also a sign of renewal and rebirth in many cultures due to its ability to bloom for long periods and resist wear from foot traffic. It’s commonly used to symbolize a fresh start or moving forward from a period of grief.

Daylily

The day lily is a flower that represents the sacrifice that a mother makes for her child. It can also represent the filial devotion that a child has to his or her own mother. The day lily also serves as a symbol of flirtation. Because it blooms at sunrise and closes at sunset, it shows that it doesn’t intend to stay around long. a white daylily symbolizes innocence, purity, perfection, and honesty. It is frequently associated with the Virgin Mary and viewed as a holy or religious flower Orange or yellow daylilies are most often used to symbolize joy, love, courage, beauty, and devotion. A darker orange daily – or even one that is redder – could symbolize deeper passion.

Geranium

Geranium's meanings are: Expected meeting, Unexpected meeting, Bridal favor, foolishenss, melancholy, Ingenuity, and Friendship. To some, the Geranium flower was a symbol of something positive and others saw it as something negative. The geranium flower is a component of many magical spells. The root of geranium was worn around the neck for protection. Geranium tea was consumed as a love potion. Brushing leaves of rose geraniums onto windows and doorknobs secured the house against intrusion. However, in certain situations and contexts, certain geraniums can also symbolize envy, deceit and folly.
